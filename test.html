PRE-GCC REPORT CHILE

BMSCE CAMPUS

CULTURES IN A GLOBALIZED WORLD – MAY 21st

This event was part one of the three Pre GCC activities based on the themes of the GCC 2016 to be held in Temuco, Chile. Fellows gathered at a JF’s house and participated in the activity. We discussed the various facets of Indian culture and how we could showcase it at the GCC. Akshay Nelakurti led us through the activity and asked our opinions about Indian culture. We spoke about many ideas and how we could go about portraying them. We decided to put up stalls, four in number, one each for food, clothing, architecture and arts, from various parts of India. We plan to do this through display of pictures.

GENDER EMPOWERMENT – JULY 12th

In our second pre GCC activity, we watched a TED talk by Adithi Gupta about menstruation being a taboo in India. As Adithi went on to explain how she was forced to use rags as sanitary napkins and how certain religious beliefs in India are binding and rigorous, the Fellows were astounded by the sheer amount of ignorance.

The Fellows were then asked to write their initial thoughts after watching the video. A flurry of thoughts, ranging from how a comic strip could effectively be used to challenge societal norms, to the courage of a woman like Adithi to stand up for millions of girls, came up. The Fellows then wrote down 5 words from the talk that stood out to them which was followed by a discussion of experiences they've had, growing up with customs and gender based discrimination in India.

The evening was then concluded, with everyone leaving the room with more open, enlightened minds.

SUSTAINABLE ECONOMIES – AUGUST 1st

As part of the third Pre GCC activity, we first watched a TED talk which highlights the amount of waste generated by people from various parts of the world and how this contributes to our carbon footprint, affects economy and how it gives rise to unhealthy, costly lifestyle. The video gave us an insight into how these bad conditions affected the speaker and how she went on to inculcating a sustainable lifestyle by reducing the amount of trash everyday and finally a message to viewers which appealed at following at least some of the discussed methods and paying a little more attention towards what we buy, use and discard.

As part of the activity we all deliberated on the various sustainable methods we could implement, why they are effective and how important it is to impart the message and inform people about the importance of sustainability. We each took a sheet of paper and wrote down a few things which we purchased which contained plastic and a few others which didn’t and then discussed about how we could reduce the former. And later, we all decided upon extending this activity to a few other people and compare the listings and discuss the pros and cons of the same.
