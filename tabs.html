NEXUS GLOBAL YOUTH SUMMIT

Report By Ashitha Nayak


The Melton Foundation was recently invited to participate in the Nexus Global Youth Summit held at the U.N Headquarters in New York, from 20th-22nd July. It was a great honour for us to be selected to the Nexus Summit and this certainly did support the efforts to build visibility and help put Melton on the map.

Nexus is a global movement which seeks to bridge communities of wealth and social entrepreneurship (enterprises). With thousands of members from over 70 countries, Nexus work to unite young investors, social entrepreneurs, philanthropists and allies to catalyze new leadership and accelerate global solutions. Founded in 2011, Nexus has hosted over 20 summits across six continents to connect young people from diverse backgrounds and link communities that would otherwise never meet. Nexus also conducts research and provides thought leadership to facilitate collaboration and build a global culture of philanthropy.

On 20th of July, 2016 , we set foot into Nexus, to get inspired and build collaborations.


July 20, 2016

We ( Winthrop, Beryl and I ) got to the New School, NYC at 2pm and attended a session on 'Pushing Boundaries through film and media' where we experienced the actual situation of Syrian Refugees through Virtual Reality. We also interacted first hand with 8-10 social impact companies that use VR, media, and audio-video to bring about change. Some of these were, 'Gasland', 'FlyByLight', 'IAmEvidence' and so on. We brainstormed ideas on how we can all collaborate to improve ways to bring impact.

At 5 pm

Victor and Gaurav joined us and we headed to the Inaugural, NEW TO NEXUS session. At New to Nexus, we were introduced to the Nexus family, what kind of engagements Nexus has, and what we can expect for the next couple of days. We then headed to a Wine and cheese reception, and then the Plenary session.

Speakers at the Plenary:

This Plenary session had possibly the most insightful, inspiring and young innovators we had seen. From Yeonmi Park who escaped from North Korea, and the 13 year old genius-inventor Max Loughan, we were spellbound at the end of it all.

After exchanging cards and brainstorming about MF's involvement with some of these companies, there was the Climate Change Working Group Dinner – an informal meet to discuss climate based projects by all interested Summit participants.

July 21, 2016

The second day of the summit was less listening, and a lot more doing. We were involved with interactive working groups, other fellowships, and speakers all day and also attended workshops that basically changed the way I think. The day was called ‘Big Ideas for a Better World’ and it started with a performance by Christen Lien.

We had the CEO of Atlas Corps talk about his fellowship institute and how they function. After this, we had discussions about mental health, Moral leadership, Global Citizenship ( we got to meet Hugh Evans – CEO, Global Citizen ) and Equal Justice.

We then broke into working groups and went ahead to different sessions. These sessions were where attendees put ideas on the table about ‘How Change Makers Start’, ‘Impact Investing’, ‘Networking across boundaries’ to name a few.

The day came to an end with a brilliant ‘prototyping workshop’ that taught us crisis management and problem solving.

Some of the people we met there are remarkable and we hope that both the Melton Network and them can benefit from our relationship.

July 22, 2016 : United Nations Day

This was a day I will probably never forget. We were seated inside possible one of the most powerful rooms in the world, and the conferences, the discussions on that day were meta-level. The day was called ‘Inspiration to Action’, named so correctly.

We had Pulitzer Prize winning journalists, technical geniuses, UN staff, and global youth activists in the room to address us.

We discussed, Sustainable Development Goals, Grassroot level Innovation, Amplifying our power and Social Impact for the entire day. Not a minute of it was tiring.

The Nexus Summit came to an end with tears and hugs and lessons and laughter. At Nexus, not only did they teach us how to network and collaborate, but they taught us that we would all have to work together.

On the first day of the summit everyone kept saying to each other that they ‘had each other’s back’. By the time the summit ended, I knew they all meant it. This summit is going to bring some great opportunities to Melton and I am so honoured that I harboured that process.

PRE-GCC REPORT CHILE

BMSCE CAMPUS

CULTURES IN A GLOBALIZED WORLD – MAY 21st

This event was part one of the three Pre GCC activities based on the themes of the GCC 2016 to be held in Temuco, Chile. Fellows gathered at a JF’s house and participated in the activity. We discussed the various facets of Indian culture and how we could showcase it at the GCC. Akshay Nelakurti led us through the activity and asked our opinions about Indian culture. We spoke about many ideas and how we could go about portraying them. We decided to put up stalls, four in number, one each for food, clothing, architecture and arts, from various parts of India. We plan to do this through display of pictures.

GENDER EMPOWERMENT – JULY 12th

In our second pre GCC activity, we watched a TED talk by Adithi Gupta about menstruation being a taboo in India. As Adithi went on to explain how she was forced to use rags as sanitary napkins and how certain religious beliefs in India are binding and rigorous, the Fellows were astounded by the sheer amount of ignorance.

The Fellows were then asked to write their initial thoughts after watching the video. A flurry of thoughts, ranging from how a comic strip could effectively be used to challenge societal norms, to the courage of a woman like Adithi to stand up for millions of girls, came up. The Fellows then wrote down 5 words from the talk that stood out to them which was followed by a discussion of experiences they've had, growing up with customs and gender based discrimination in India.

The evening was then concluded, with everyone leaving the room with more open, enlightened minds.

SUSTAINABLE ECONOMIES – AUGUST 1st

As part of the third Pre GCC activity, we first watched a TED talk which highlights the amount of waste generated by people from various parts of the world and how this contributes to our carbon footprint, affects economy and how it gives rise to unhealthy, costly lifestyle. The video gave us an insight into how these bad conditions affected the speaker and how she went on to inculcating a sustainable lifestyle by reducing the amount of trash everyday and finally a message to viewers which appealed at following at least some of the discussed methods and paying a little more attention towards what we buy, use and discard.

As part of the activity we all deliberated on the various sustainable methods we could implement, why they are effective and how important it is to impart the message and inform people about the importance of sustainability. We each took a sheet of paper and wrote down a few things which we purchased which contained plastic and a few others which didn’t and then discussed about how we could reduce the former. And later, we all decided upon extending this activity to a few other people and compare the listings and discuss the pros and cons of the same.

NABC: From powerful ideas to solid value propositions by Victor Valenzuela (Chile)

In the business world,several tools are used to structure and strengthen ideas so they can be successfully implemented. This workshop will help you understand and apply one of these tools; a method to make your social innovative idea evolve into a solid, actionable, easy to transmit, human-centered-design based Value Proposition, thus setting the core and paving the way for a global problem solving project or a social startup.

User Research: Beyond engineering and into design by Pedro Poblete Lasserre (Chile)

Engineers make stuff, but it is everyday people who use it. Think of your grandparents or oldest uncle/aunt. Now think about them setting up a wireless printer. Can you see the gap? Human- Technology Interaction tries to understand the psychological, technical and contextual factors that affect the relationship between people and technology.

In this workshop, we’ll learn the basics of user-centred research, moving beyond requirements and architectures, and towards a better understanding of the people we develop for and their personal relationship with our systems.

Popular Technologies: challenging the conventional by Dr. Karla Palma (Chile)

Technologies are usually described from a global dominant perspective, but how do particular local contexts and knowledge influence the development of technologies? In this workshop we will analyze, from a critical perspective,scenarios of precariousness where resistant communities develop new knowledge and skills to transform their realities. Then we will explore how some methodologiesthat are typically used by social scientist can be useful for engineers, who want to engage local communitiesin the development of unconventional technologiesto help solve local needs.

Sustainability: Looking under the hood by Julian Klauke (Germany)

Everybody has a rough idea about what sustainability means and which products and technologies are sustainable. In this workshop, we will to take a closer look and examine in detail whether what we *think we* know can be correct, how complex sustainability really is and how the human factor plays an important role in the net effect of sustainable products.

Engineers Needed: Creating Innovative Solutions For People with Disabilities by Alafia Stewart (USA)

Did you know that roughly ten percent of people on the planet are living with a disability? Many of them have physical limitations and rely on assistive technologies every day. Engineers are redefining the concept of limitations and we invite you to be apart of the process. By taking an exploratory approach to awarenesssurrounding various types of disabilitiesthrough interactive activities, let's discuss, collaborate, and design assistive technologiesfor everyday use. Are you up for the challenge?

Big Data: from Data Explosion to Data Exploration by Yandi Shen (China) & Rajath R Dani (India)

Data containsinformation, which allows us to make sense of how this world functions. Tremendous amounts of data prevailed in the era of “big data” poses great challenge in data storage and computation, but also motivatesthe developmentsin areas such as data mining or machine learning which can innovate societies, ecosystems, or technologies. In this workshop, we will explore what it means to think as data scientiststhrough a systematic introduction, interactive live demonstrations and a hands-on projects.
